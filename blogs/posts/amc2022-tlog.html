2022 AMC 10B Travel Log

On Nov. 16, I participated in 2022 AMC 10 B at Oyster River High School, here's my feeling and some solutions of the contest.

Problem 6

My performance is well until I met problem 6. "How many of the first ten numbers of the sequence \(121, 11211, 1112111, \ldots\) are prime numbers?" I was about to try them one by one but I thought if I continue trying, it won't have enough time for the remaining problem, so I have to give up. Until I found the solution that is \[ \forall n\in\mathbb{Z}^+,\ \frac{10^{2n+2}-1}{9}+10^{n}=\frac{10^{n+1}-1}{9}(10^n+1) \] Therefore nothing is prime.

Problem 9

Then, I stuck again at problem 9: Find \[ \frac{1}{2!}+\frac{2}{3!}+\frac{3}{4!}+\dots+\frac{2021}{2022!} \] I tried many ways to transform the equation, and get itself in the end, so I gave up at the problem. Until I saw the solution: Note that \[ \frac{n}{(n+1)!} = \frac{1}{n!} - \frac{1}{(n+1)!} \] after that the problem can be easily solved. Both solutions of the problem aren't very long, but they both need a discovery. I didn't discovered these things, so I failed to solve these problems.

Problem 12

It finally came to my favorite probability problem. I easily found that rolling a pair of fair $6$-sided dice, the probability of getting a sum of 7 is \(\frac16\). The probability of not getting a sum of 7 is \(1-\frac16=\frac56\). Rolling the pair of dice $n$ times, the probability of getting a sum of 7 at least once is \[ 1-\left(\frac56\right)^n. \] Therefore, we have \[ 1-\left(\frac56\right)^n>\frac12, \] so the smallest value of $n$ is 4.

Problem 15

This is another problem that I stuck at. I uses formula \[ S_n = \frac{(a_1+a_n)n}{2}, \] but I incorrectly used the formula \[ n=\frac{a_n-a_1}{d}+1, \] so I gave up after getting a lot of difficult stuff. Actually I only need to find that \(S_{n} = n(a + n - 1) = n^2 + n(a - 1)\). Then I only need to find \[ \frac{S_{3n}}{S_{n}} = \frac{9n^2 + 3n(a - 1)}{n^2 + n(a - 1)} = 9 - \frac{6n(a-1)}{n^2 + n(a-1)}. \] then it can be easily solved.
