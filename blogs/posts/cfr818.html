Codeforces Round 818 (Div. 2)

Recently, I've joined Codeforces Round 818 (Div. 2). The round contains a lot of good math problem. Here's my feelings and solutions of them.

A

If a pair of integers \( (a,b) \) satisfies \( \frac{\text{lcm(a,b)}}{\gcd(a,b)}\leq 3 \), one of \(a=b\), \(a=2b\), \(a=3b\), \(b=2a\), \(b=3a\) must be satisfied.

e.g. if \( a=\frac{3}{2}b \), assume \(a=3k\), \(b=2k\), then \( \frac{\text{lcm(a,b)}}{\gcd(a,b)}=\frac{6k}{k}=6>3 \).

Then, the answer is obviously \[ n+2\left(\left\lfloor\frac{n}{2}\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor\frac{n}{3}\right\rfloor\right) \]

B

Let's first ignore the limit of \(r\) and \(c\), and we can output \(\texttt{X}\) at \((x,y)\) if \[x\bmod k=y\bmod k\] Then add the limit of \((r,c)\), we can get that we can output \(\texttt{X}\) at \((x,y)\) if \[y\bmod k-c\bmod k+k\equiv (x-1)\bmod k-r\bmod k+k+1\pmod k\]
